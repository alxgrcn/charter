# VA Caregiver Support Program

## Program Overview
VA's Caregiver Support Program provides dedicated resources, training, and mental health support to caregivers of eligible veterans. Supporting the caregiver is part of supporting the veteran's own recovery.

Two primary programs:

Program of Comprehensive Assistance for Family Caregivers (PCAFC):
- Monthly stipend paid to the caregiver
- Healthcare coverage for the caregiver (through CHAMPVA if not otherwise covered)
- Mental health counseling and peer support for the caregiver
- Respite care (temporary relief from caregiving duties)
- Caregiver training and education
- 24/7 Caregiver Support Line

Program of General Caregiver Support Services (PGCSS):
- Available for caregivers of veterans of all eras
- Caregiver education and training
- Support groups and peer connection
- Connections to VA and community resources
- Access to the 24/7 Caregiver Support Line

## Who This May Help
- Veterans who have a family member or close friend helping them manage daily life or mental health challenges
- Caregivers experiencing stress, burnout, or mental health strain from their caregiving role
- Families navigating PTSD, TBI, or other serious mental health conditions together

## Eligibility for the Veteran (PCAFC)
- Must have a serious injury — including PTSD or TBI — incurred in the line of duty
- Must be discharged from military service
- Must need in-person personal care services for 6+ continuous months
- Must be enrolled in VA Healthcare

PGCSS is available for veterans of all eras enrolled in VA Healthcare.

## Eligibility for the Caregiver
- Must be at least 18 years old
- Must be a family member or live with the veteran as if family

## Cost
PGCSS is free. PCAFC provides a stipend and healthcare to the caregiver.

## How to Access
1. Call the VA Caregiver Support Line: 1-855-260-3274 (Mon–Fri 8am–10pm ET; Sat 8am–5pm ET)
2. Visit caregiver.va.gov
3. Contact the Caregiver Support Coordinator at the nearest VA Medical Center

## Key Message for Veterans and Families
If someone in your life has been helping you manage since leaving service — a spouse, parent, sibling, or close friend — VA has resources specifically for them too.
