# VA Substance Use Disorder (SUD) Treatment

## Program Overview
The VA provides comprehensive substance use disorder treatment as part of its standard mental health benefits package. SUD treatment is available to eligible veterans at no cost. Alcohol use disorder, drug use disorder, prescription medication misuse, and co-occurring mental health and substance use conditions are all addressed.

Treatment options include:
- Outpatient individual and group SUD counseling
- Intensive Outpatient Programs (IOP)
- Medically supervised detoxification (inpatient or residential)
- Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT) for opioid use disorder and alcohol use disorder
- Residential SUD rehabilitation programs
- Dual diagnosis treatment for co-occurring SUD and PTSD, depression, or other conditions
- Telehealth SUD counseling

## Who This May Help
Veterans who:
- Are using alcohol or drugs to cope with stress, trauma, sleep problems, or pain
- Feel their use is getting harder to control
- Have been told by family or friends that their use is a concern
- Have tried to cut back and found it difficult
- Are experiencing withdrawal symptoms
- Are open to exploring whether substance use has become a problem

## Eligibility
- Veterans enrolled in VA Healthcare
- Any discharge status — including OTH discharge veterans
- OTH veterans who served at least 100 days in a combat role are eligible for mental and behavioral health care, which includes SUD treatment
- Emergency SUD stabilization available to OTH veterans presenting in crisis

VA is legally required to provide SUD treatment as part of its standard benefits package.

## Cost
- First 3 SUD outpatient visits per calendar year: free through December 2027
- Veterans with 10%+ service-connected disability: no copay
- No income means test required for SUD treatment access

## 42 CFR Part 2 — Privacy Rights for SUD Disclosures
Federal law (42 CFR Part 2) provides extra protection for substance use disorder treatment records. SUD treatment records at VA are protected and cannot be shared with other organizations — including employers, law enforcement, or other agencies — without explicit written consent. This is stronger protection than standard HIPAA.

If a veteran shares information about substance use when connecting with a veteran support organization, they have the right to specify whether that information is shared further. Consent must name the specific organization receiving the information.

## How to Access in California
1. Enroll in VA Healthcare at va.gov/health-care/apply or call 1-877-222-8387
2. Contact nearest VAMC and ask for the SUD clinic or substance use treatment team
3. Request same-day mental health services and mention substance use concerns
4. Ask about Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT) if opioid use is a concern

## Key Message for Veterans
Substance use in veterans is often a coping response to experiences during service — not a moral failure. VA's SUD teams approach care without judgment. Treatment is available and covered, and your privacy is protected by federal law.
