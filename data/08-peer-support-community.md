# Peer Support and Community Programs — California and US Vets

## Program Overview
Peer support and community-based programs provide human connection, practical navigation support, and a bridge to clinical services for veterans who are not ready for or do not qualify for formal VA programs.

---

## US Vets — California Programs

US Veterans Inc. (US Vets) is a California-based nonprofit providing housing, mental health counseling, career development, and community support to veterans across Southern California.

US Vets Mental Health Services:
- Individual and group counseling — free, available regardless of VA enrollment or discharge status
- Substance use treatment — individual and group sessions
- Women Vets on Point — mental health counseling and wellness programming specifically for women veterans, addressing trauma, MST, and challenges unique to female veterans
- Case management — wraparound support to help veterans navigate VA and community resources

US Vets California Locations:
- Los Angeles — Bob Hope Patriotic Hall, 1816 S. Figueroa Street, Los Angeles, CA
- Inland Empire — March Veterans Village, Riverside / San Bernardino counties
- Contact: usvets.org

---

## VA Peer Support Specialists
Within VA facilities, Peer Support Specialists are veterans with lived experience of mental health recovery. They:
- Share their own experience to inspire hope and reduce stigma
- Help veterans understand treatment options in plain terms
- Connect veterans to services they may not know about
- Available through referral from a VA healthcare provider

---

## BeThere Peer Assistance Program
For veterans within 365 days of discharge and their families:
- Free, confidential peer coaching by phone
- Coaches are veterans, service members, or military spouses
- Call Military OneSource: 1-800-342-9647 (24/7)
- No VA enrollment required

---

## Veteran Peer Access Network (VPAN) — Los Angeles County
- Operated with LA County Department of Mental Health
- Connects veterans to community resources regardless of discharge status
- Located at Veterans AJCC, Bob Hope Patriotic Hall, LA
- Contact: (213) 742-9544

---

## Vet Center 24/7 Call Center
- Call: 1-877-WAR-VETS (1-877-927-8387)
- Available 24/7, staffed by veterans and family members of veterans

---

## Who These Programs May Help
Veterans who:
- Are not yet enrolled in VA Healthcare but want support now
- Have an OTH or other discharge creating uncertainty about VA eligibility
- Are exploring options before committing to a clinical program
- Want to speak with another veteran who has navigated similar challenges
- Need case management help to figure out which programs to pursue
- Are recently separated and feeling uncertain about civilian life

## Cost
All peer support and community programs listed here are free of charge.

## Key Message for Veterans
You do not need a diagnosis, a VA card, or a specific discharge to get support. Peer support exists for the veteran who isn't sure where to start.
