# Crisis Resources — Veterans Crisis Line and Emergency Mental Health

## IMPORTANT: If a Veteran Is in Crisis Right Now
**Call or text 988 and press 1**
The Veterans Crisis Line is available 24 hours a day, 7 days a week.
Text: 838255
Chat: veteranscrisisline.net
TTY: Dial 711, then 988

This line is free, confidential, and staffed by trained responders — many of whom are veterans themselves.

---

## Veterans Crisis Line — 988 (Press 1)
The Veterans Crisis Line is the primary national crisis resource for veterans and service members. It is available to ALL veterans and service members, regardless of:
- Discharge status (including OTH)
- VA enrollment status
- Era of service
- Whether they are currently receiving VA care

Responders are trained specifically to support veterans. The line is confidential. Calling or texting does not automatically involve law enforcement unless there is immediate danger.

---

## COMPACT Act — Emergency Suicidal Crisis Care
The Veterans Comprehensive Prevention, Access to Care, and Treatment (COMPACT) Act allows eligible veterans to receive emergency mental health care at ANY VA or non-VA emergency facility at no cost if they are experiencing an acute suicidal crisis.

Key facts:
- No VA enrollment required
- No prior authorization required
- Available at any emergency room — VA or community
- No-cost emergency care including transportation, inpatient or crisis residential care for up to 30 days, and outpatient follow-up for up to 90 days
- Available to veterans discharged under conditions other than dishonorable who served more than 24 months on active duty

Since the COMPACT Act took effect, more than 60,000 veterans have used this benefit.

---

## Walk-In Emergency Mental Health — VA Medical Centers
Any VA Medical Center provides emergency mental health care 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. Veterans can walk in without an appointment and without prior enrollment if they are in crisis.

For OTH discharge veterans: VA is required to provide emergency mental health stabilization to veterans presenting at any VAMC with an urgent mental health need, regardless of discharge status. Initial stabilization care can last up to 90 days.

## California Mobile Crisis Resources
California counties operate mobile crisis units that can come to a veteran in crisis at home or in the community — without involving law enforcement in many situations.

- LA County Department of Mental Health Crisis Line: 1-800-854-7771 (24/7)
- CalHOPE (statewide): calhope.dhcs.ca.gov
- 988 California: routes to California-specific crisis resources when available

---

## What Happens After a Crisis Contact
If a veteran uses the Veterans Crisis Line or walks into a VA emergency, a Suicide Prevention Coordinator at the nearest VA Medical Center may follow up to connect them to ongoing care. This is not punitive — it is a support function.

Veterans who receive emergency care under the COMPACT Act will be offered a referral to longer-term VA or community mental health care as part of their discharge from the emergency setting.

---

## Key Message
If things feel unmanageable right now, please call 988 and press 1. The people on that line are there specifically for veterans, and the call is confidential. Getting support in a crisis moment is exactly what these resources are designed for.
