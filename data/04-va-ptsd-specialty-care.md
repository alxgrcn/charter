# VA PTSD Specialty Care

## Program Overview
The VA operates dedicated PTSD Clinical Teams and specialty programs at VA Medical Centers nationwide, including throughout California. These teams provide evidence-based PTSD treatment from providers specifically trained in trauma-focused therapies.

Treatment options include:
- Cognitive Processing Therapy (CPT)
- Prolonged Exposure (PE)
- EMDR (Eye Movement Desensitization and Reprocessing)
- Medication management for PTSD symptoms
- PTSD-specific group therapy programs
- Telehealth PTSD treatment (available statewide in California)
- Residential PTSD programs at select VA facilities

## Who This May Help
Veterans experiencing:
- Flashbacks, nightmares, or intrusive memories related to combat or military service
- Hypervigilance, sleep problems, or being easily startled
- Emotional numbing, withdrawal from family or friends
- Avoiding reminders of traumatic events
- Anger, irritability, or difficulty concentrating
- Feeling hopeless or detached since leaving service
- PTSD symptoms related to military sexual trauma

Veterans do NOT need a formal PTSD diagnosis before seeking care.

## Eligibility
- Veterans enrolled in VA Healthcare
- No formal diagnosis required to begin — evaluation and diagnosis happen through VA
- Any era of service
- OTH discharge veterans: eligible for emergency mental health care including PTSD stabilization for up to 90 days; may qualify for ongoing care

## OTH Discharge and PTSD
Veterans with OTH discharges are encouraged to apply. If the OTH discharge was connected to behavior caused by PTSD during service, a discharge upgrade may be possible. VA's compelling circumstances exception specifically names PTSD as a basis for upgrade consideration. This does not need to be resolved before seeking care.

## Cost
- First 3 outpatient PTSD visits per calendar year: free through December 2027
- Veterans with 10%+ service-connected disability: no copay
- PTSD residential programs: covered under VA Healthcare enrollment

## How to Access in California
1. Enroll in VA Healthcare (if not already) at va.gov/health-care/apply
2. Contact nearest VAMC or CBOC and ask for a referral to the PTSD Clinical Team
3. Request same-day mental health services for an immediate evaluation
4. Telehealth options available statewide

## National PTSD Resource
VA's National Center for PTSD — ptsd.va.gov

## Key Message for Veterans
PTSD is not a character flaw or a sign of weakness. It is a known medical response to extreme experiences, and it is treatable. You don't need a formal diagnosis before reaching out.
