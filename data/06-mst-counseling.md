# Military Sexual Trauma (MST) Counseling

## Program Overview
The VA provides free mental health treatment for any physical or mental health condition related to military sexual trauma (MST). MST-related care is available regardless of discharge status, length of service, or whether the MST was ever reported when it happened.

VA defines MST as sexual assault or threatening sexual harassment experienced during military service. This includes:
- Any sexual activity during service that occurred without consent or where consent could not be freely given
- Being pressured or coerced into sexual activities
- Sexual contact while asleep or intoxicated
- Unwanted touching or grabbing
- Threatening or demeaning sexual comments
- Sexual hazing experiences

MST can happen to veterans of any gender, any branch, any era, and any rank.

## Treatment Available
- Individual therapy and counseling (outpatient)
- Group therapy for MST survivors
- Residential or inpatient programs for more intensive treatment
- MST-related care at community-based Vet Centers
- Evidence-based trauma therapies including CPT, Prolonged Exposure, and EMDR
- Request to work with a provider of a specific gender — always honored
- MST Coordinator at every VA Medical Facility

## Who This May Help
Veterans who experienced sexual assault or sexual harassment during military service and are experiencing:
- PTSD symptoms related to the experience
- Depression, anxiety, or emotional numbness
- Difficulty with relationships or trust
- Substance use that began or increased after the MST
- Avoidance of situations that are reminders of the experience
- Any health concerns — physical or mental — that started or worsened after the MST

You do NOT need to have reported the MST when it happened. You do NOT need any documentation. You do NOT need a VA disability rating.

## Eligibility — Highly Inclusive
MST-related care has the most expansive eligibility rules in VA mental health:
- Veterans with honorable or general (under honorable conditions) discharge
- Veterans with an Other Than Honorable (OTH) discharge — eligible without needing a service-connected disability
- Veterans who served fewer than two years
- Veterans of any era of service
- Current active duty service members (at Vet Centers without DoD referral)
- Guard and Reserve members with federal active-duty service

Standard length-of-service requirements do not apply to MST-related care.

## No Documentation Required
You do not need a police report, military service records, a VA disability rating, or proof the MST was reported at the time.

## Cost
MST-related treatment is provided free of charge. No copay required.

## How to Access in California
1. Contact nearest VA Medical Center and ask to speak with the MST Coordinator
2. Contact nearest Vet Center and ask for MST-related counseling — no appointment required
3. Call 1-800-827-1000 Monday–Friday, 8am–9pm ET
4. For women veterans: Women Veterans Call Center at 855-829-6636

## Key Message for Veterans
What happened to you was not your fault. VA provides care specifically for MST survivors, without judgment, without required documentation, and without needing to relive the experience at intake.
