# VA Residential Mental Health Programs (RRTP)

## Program Overview
The VA operates Mental Health Residential Rehabilitation Treatment Programs (RRTPs) for veterans who need a higher level of mental health support than outpatient care alone can provide. These are live-in programs where veterans receive structured treatment while residing at the facility.

RRTPs take a whole-health approach, addressing:
- Mental health treatment (PTSD, depression, anxiety, trauma)
- Substance use disorder treatment
- Medical concerns
- Social needs such as employment and housing stability

## Types of Residential Programs
- General RRTP — for veterans with serious mental illness, PTSD, or complex trauma
- PTSD-specific residential programs — intensive PTSD treatment, available at select VA facilities
- Women-only residential programs — available at select facilities with separate living areas
- Domiciliary Care — longer-term residential rehabilitation for veterans who lack stable housing necessary for recovery

## Who This May Help
Veterans experiencing:
- Serious, persistent mental illness with functional impairment
- PTSD severe enough to make outpatient care insufficient
- Mental health conditions combined with housing instability
- Mental health conditions requiring more intensive support than weekly therapy

## Eligibility
- Must be enrolled in VA Healthcare
- Must have a mental health condition such as PTSD, depression, or substance use disorder
- Must require more intensive support than outpatient care provides
- For domiciliary programs: must lack stable housing necessary for recovery
- Must NOT currently need acute inpatient psychiatric or medical care

## OTH Discharge Note
OTH veterans who present at a VAMC in an emergency mental health crisis may be stabilized in inpatient or residential care for up to 90 days. After 90 days, continued need is reassessed.

## Cost
Covered under VA Healthcare benefits for enrolled veterans. Copay may apply based on priority group.

## How to Access in California
Residential programs require a referral from a VA mental health provider, or through emergency mental health intake.
1. Contact nearest VA Medical Center and request a mental health evaluation
2. Ask specifically about residential treatment options
3. Walk-in to any VAMC emergency mental health services — same-day evaluation available

## California Facilities with Residential Programs
- VA Greater Los Angeles Healthcare System
- VA Loma Linda Healthcare System
- VA San Diego Healthcare System
- VA Northern California (Sacramento / Martinez)

## Key Message for Veterans
If weekly therapy hasn't been enough, or if your mental health challenges are making it hard to stay stable at home, residential treatment may be an option worth exploring with a VA provider.
