HUD-VASH (HUD-Veterans Affairs Supportive Housing) Program — Veteran Eligibility Guide

Source: U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development / U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs
Program Authority: 42 U.S.C. 1437f(o)(19); 38 U.S.C. Chapter 20

SECTION 1: WHO IS ELIGIBLE FOR HUD-VASH

To be eligible for a HUD-VASH voucher, a veteran must meet ALL of the following criteria:

1. Veteran status: The individual must be a veteran as defined under 38 U.S.C. § 101(2) — that is, a person who served in the active military, naval, or air service and was discharged or released under conditions other than dishonorable.

2. Homeless or at-risk of homelessness status: The veteran must currently be:
   a. Literally homeless — living on the streets, in a shelter, or in a place not meant for human habitation; OR
   b. In transitional housing — residing in a transitional housing program after a period of homelessness; OR
   c. At imminent risk of homelessness — likely to lose their primary nighttime residence within 14 days; OR
   d. Fleeing domestic violence or other dangerous situations without a safe alternative place to stay.

   NOTE: Veterans currently residing in transitional housing are considered to have "homeless history" and are among the highest-priority populations for HUD-VASH referral. VA case managers actively refer veterans in transitional housing settings to HUD-VASH as a permanent housing solution.

3. VA healthcare eligibility: The veteran must be eligible for VA healthcare services (see VA healthcare eligibility criteria). VA case managers provide the case management services required by the HUD-VASH program.

4. Clinical assessment: The veteran must participate in a clinical assessment conducted by a VA case manager to determine appropriateness for the program and level of case management needed.

SECTION 2: PRIORITY POPULATIONS

HUD-VASH prioritizes veterans who are:
- Chronically homeless (homeless for 12+ months or 4+ times in the last 3 years with a disabling condition)
- Currently in transitional housing after a period of literal homelessness
- At-risk of returning to homelessness after transitional housing placement ends
- Veterans with disability ratings who face barriers to stable housing

Veterans with ANY disability rating (10%, 20%, 30%, or higher) who are in transitional housing are particularly well-suited for HUD-VASH because the program combines rental assistance with VA case management, addressing both housing instability and ongoing service needs.

SECTION 3: DISCHARGE STATUS REQUIREMENTS

Veterans with Honorable or General Under Honorable Conditions discharges are fully eligible. Veterans with Other Than Honorable (OTH) discharges may be eligible following a Character of Discharge review by VA.

SECTION 4: INCOME AND RENT

HUD-VASH vouchers are administered by local Public Housing Authorities (PHAs). Participant rent contribution is typically 30% of adjusted gross income. There is no minimum income requirement — veterans with very low or no income are eligible.

SECTION 5: HOW TO APPLY

Veterans should contact their nearest VA Medical Center (VAMC) and ask for the HUD-VASH coordinator or social work department. VA case managers coordinate referrals and eligibility determination. Veterans in transitional housing should ask their transitional housing program staff to facilitate a warm referral to the local VA HUD-VASH team.

Contact: 1-800-827-1000 (VA main line) or visit va.gov/homeless/hud-vash.asp
