# VA Mental Health Outpatient Services

## Program Overview
The VA provides a broad range of outpatient mental health services to eligible veterans at VA Medical Centers (VAMCs), Community-Based Outpatient Clinics (CBOCs), and through telehealth. This is the primary access point for veteran mental health care and serves as the gateway to more specialized programs.

Services available include:
- Individual therapy and counseling
- Group therapy
- Medication management
- Primary care–mental health integration (same-day mental health within primary care visits)
- Telehealth and video appointments from home
- Same-day mental health services (walk-in available at most facilities)
- Phone-based mental health consultation (nurse triage)
- Secure messaging with a VA provider

## Who This May Help
Veterans who are enrolled or eligible to enroll in VA Healthcare and are experiencing:
- Depression or anxiety
- PTSD or trauma-related symptoms
- Grief and loss
- Relationship and family stress
- Difficulty adjusting to civilian life
- Sleep problems linked to service experience
- Any mental health concern connected to military service

## Eligibility
- Veterans enrolled in VA Healthcare, OR
- Veterans who meet minimum service requirements (generally 24 continuous months of active duty, with many exceptions) and were discharged under conditions other than dishonorable
- Combat veterans discharged after November 11, 1998 are eligible for free care for 10 years post-discharge without income means test
- OTH discharge veterans: eligible for emergency mental health care; may also be eligible for ongoing care depending on circumstances

## OTH Discharge Note
Veterans with an Other Than Honorable (OTH) discharge are encouraged to apply. VA will review each case individually. OTH veterans retain access to:
- Emergency mental health stabilization at any VAMC, for up to 90 days
- Mental and behavioral health care if they served at least 100 days and in a combat theater
- Emergency suicidal crisis care at any VA or non-VA facility at no cost (COMPACT Act)
- MST-related care regardless of discharge status

## Cost
- First 3 outpatient mental health visits per calendar year: free through December 2027
- Veterans with 10%+ service-connected disability rating: no copay for outpatient care
- Other veterans: copay varies by priority group
- Emergency suicidal crisis care: always free, regardless of enrollment or discharge status

## How to Access in California
1. Enroll in VA Healthcare at va.gov/health-care/apply or call 1-877-222-8387
2. Contact nearest VA Medical Center or CBOC in California
3. Ask for same-day mental health services or schedule an outpatient mental health appointment
4. Telehealth options available statewide — no travel required

## California VA Facilities (Key Locations)
- VA Greater Los Angeles Healthcare System — Los Angeles
- VA Long Beach Healthcare System — Long Beach
- VA Loma Linda Healthcare System — Loma Linda
- VA San Diego Healthcare System — San Diego
- VA Northern California Health Care System — Sacramento / Martinez
- Multiple CBOCs across Los Angeles, Orange, Riverside, San Bernardino, and Ventura counties

## Key Message for Veterans
Reaching out is the first step, and VA can usually connect you with someone the same day you call. You do not need to have a formal diagnosis to ask for mental health support. If you are not sure whether you qualify, apply anyway — VA encourages all veterans to apply regardless of discharge status.
